🌍 Global Population Trends Analysis (1970–2022)
📌 Project Overview

This project analyzes global population data from 1970 to 2022 to uncover key trends, patterns, and insights. The goal is to understand how the world population has evolved over time and identify shifts in growth rates across different countries.

🎯 Objectives

Analyze global population growth over the last five decades

Identify the most populated countries in 2022

Study the distribution of population growth rates

Derive meaningful insights for future implications

📊 Key Insights
🌍 World Population Growth

The global population has steadily increased from under 4 billion (1970) to ~8 billion (2022)

Rapid growth observed between 1970–2010

Post-2010, growth continues but at a slower rate, indicating a gradual stabilization

🏙️ Top 5 Most Populated Countries (2022)

China and India lead with populations exceeding 1.4 billion

Followed by:

United States

Indonesia

Pakistan

Significant population gap after the top two countries

📉 Population Growth Rate Distribution

Most countries fall within 1.00–1.02, indicating stable growth

Some countries show high growth (~1.06)

A few regions exhibit declining trends (<0.94)

💡 Key Takeaways

Global population is still increasing, but growth is slowing down

Indicates a shift toward population stabilization

Important for:

Economic planning

Resource management

Policy making
